# DeepPhilosophy Timeline V2

## Museum of Philosophy Design Specification

Version 2.0

Author: Claude Code Design Specification

Target Project: DeepPhilosophy

---

# 1. Project Vision

DeepPhilosophy is **not** intended to be another philosophy encyclopedia, another educational website, or another timeline visualization.

Its purpose is to become the world's most beautiful and most comprehensive digital museum dedicated to the history of philosophy.

Users should not feel that they are browsing a webpage.

Users should feel that they are walking through humanity's intellectual civilization.

Every scroll should resemble moving through a museum gallery.

Every philosophy school should resemble a historical exhibit.

Every transition should resemble travelling between civilizations.

The website should simultaneously function as:

* Philosophy Encyclopedia
* Civilization Atlas
* Interactive Museum
* Intellectual Genealogy
* Knowledge Graph
* Digital Archive

The emotional experience should always remain calm, scholarly, timeless and elegant.

The website should never become entertainment-oriented.

The design should encourage contemplation rather than excitement.

---

# 2. Design Philosophy

The visual identity is built upon one central metaphor:

> Human thought flows like a river.

Ideas never appear independently.

Every philosophy emerges from previous civilizations.

Every school influences later generations.

Every civilization contributes to the continuous river of human thought.

Therefore the website abandons the traditional concept of a timeline.

Instead it introduces:

**The River of Philosophy**

This river becomes:

* the timeline
* the navigation
* the page structure
* the visual identity
* the storytelling device

The River of Philosophy is not decoration.

The River of Philosophy is the architecture of the page itself.

---

# 6. Non-Negotiable Principles

These rules have absolute priority.

No visual improvement is allowed to violate them.

## Principle 1

Information always has higher priority than artwork.

Artwork supports content.

Content never supports artwork.

---

## Principle 2

Every philosophy school currently contained in DeepPhilosophy must remain visible.

Nothing may be removed.

Nothing may be hidden.

Nothing may be collapsed.

Nothing may require additional clicks.

Everything remains discoverable through one continuous scroll.

---

## Principle 3

Background images may never reduce readability.

Every card must maintain sufficient contrast.

Every title must remain readable.

Every paragraph must remain readable.

If a visual effect reduces readability, remove the visual effect.

---

## Principle 4

The website is first a knowledge system.

Second a museum.

Third an artwork.

Never reverse this order.

---

## Principle 5

Scrolling represents travelling through civilization.

Users are not moving through a webpage.

Users are moving upstream along humanity's River of Thought.

---

# 7. Existing Project Constraints

The redesign must preserve all existing philosophy schools.

The current database remains unchanged.

The current relationships remain unchanged.

Only visual presentation, interaction, motion and layout may change.

The redesign must be compatible with future additions without requiring another structural redesign.

The system should comfortably scale from the current philosophy schools to several hundred schools in the future.

# 22. River Usage Rules

The page is built around the river.

Think of the river as the landscape.

# 31. Philosophy Islands

Timeline nodes should not simply connect to lines.

Each important philosophy school occupies an island.

Islands are small pieces of land beside the river.

Cards attach naturally to islands.

Major philosophy schools receive larger islands.

Minor schools receive smaller islands.

Never use plain circles.

Never use simple dots.

The islands create memorable landmarks.

---

# 32. Card Docking

Every card docks beside the river.

Imagine ancient cities built along riverbanks.

Cards never float randomly.

Every card visually belongs to the river.

Suggested connector:

Very thin bronze line.

Curved.

Never perfectly straight.

Never arrow-shaped.

---

# 33. Era Expansion

The river width should change naturally.

Major intellectual flourishing:

wider river

Examples:

Ancient Greece

Song-Ming Neo-Confucianism

Enlightenment

20th Century Philosophy

Periods of slower development:

slightly narrower.

This creates subconscious historical rhythm.

---

# 34. Cross-Civilization Interaction

Do not isolate civilizations.

Whenever two civilizations genuinely influence each other,

show a bridge.

Examples:

Greek → Islamic

Islamic → Europe

India → China

China → Japan

Europe → America

Marx → China

These interactions are among the most beautiful moments of the page.

They should receive careful visual emphasis.

---

# 35. River Safety Rules

This rule has absolute priority.

The river may NEVER reduce readability.

The river may NEVER overlap text.

The river may NEVER reduce card contrast.

Cards always remain above the river.

River z-index is always below content.

If necessary:

Fade the river away completely behind dense content.

Content always wins.

---

# 36. Continuous Landscape

Users should never feel that they are scrolling through sections.

Instead,

the page becomes one uninterrupted historical landscape.

Ancient Egypt gradually transforms into Greece.

Greece gradually transforms into Medieval Europe.

Europe gradually transforms into the Enlightenment.

The Enlightenment gradually transforms into Modern Philosophy.

Every transition should be almost imperceptible.

# 37. Scroll Narrative

Future of Philosophy.

Without reading any text,

# 38. Signature Experience

If users remember only one visual element after leaving the website,

The river should become the unmistakable visual identity of DeepPhilosophy.

# Part 12
## DeepPhilosophy Constitution
## Project Constitution
## Guiding Principles

---

# 191. Why This Project Exists

DeepPhilosophy does not exist merely to organize philosophical knowledge.

Nor does it exist simply to present historical information.

Its purpose is to reveal that human civilization is one continuous conversation.

Across thousands of years,

across continents,

across religions,

across languages,

human beings have never stopped asking the same questions.

Who are we?

What is truth?

How should we live?

What can we know?

What is freedom?

What is justice?

Every philosophy school is one answer.

Every philosopher is one participant.

DeepPhilosophy exists so that users may witness this conversation.

---

# 192. Knowledge Above Everything

Knowledge is the foundation of the project.

Beauty exists to illuminate knowledge.

Technology exists to support beauty.

Therefore:

Knowledge

↓

Beauty

↓

Technology

This order must never change.

Any feature that weakens knowledge,
regardless of how beautiful,
should be rejected.

---

# 193. Completeness

No philosophy should disappear because it is obscure.

No civilization should be ignored because it is geographically distant.

No school should be simplified because it is difficult.

DeepPhilosophy respects every sincere attempt
to understand reality.

Completeness is an act of respect.

---

# 194. Neutrality

DeepPhilosophy is not an advocate
for any philosophical position.

It neither praises
nor criticizes.

It documents.

Connects.

Explains.

Compares.

Users remain free
to reach their own conclusions.

The interface itself should never suggest
which philosophy is "correct."

---

# 195. Civilization

Civilizations should never be presented
as isolated achievements.

Ideas travel.

Ideas transform.

Ideas translate.

Ideas influence one another.

The history of philosophy
is therefore not a collection of parallel timelines.

It is one interconnected river.

---

# 196. The River of Philosophy

The River of Philosophy
is the only irreplaceable visual symbol
of DeepPhilosophy.

Everything else may evolve.

Typography.

Cards.

Animation.

Navigation.

Color.

Architecture.

Even technology.

Only one thing must remain.

The river.

The river represents:

Memory.

Inheritance.

Dialogue.

Civilization.

Continuity.

It is the visual identity
of the entire project.

---

# 197. Museum Spirit

Users should never feel
that they are consuming information.

They should feel
that they are entering
a museum built from human thought.

Every page
is another gallery.

Every school
is another exhibit.

Every philosopher
is another voice.

The museum belongs
to every civilization.

---

# 198. Respect

Every civilization deserves dignity.

Every philosophical tradition deserves careful presentation.

Ancient traditions deserve the same respect
as contemporary theories.

Large schools deserve no more honesty
than small schools.

Historical accuracy
is itself a form of respect.

---

# 199. Simplicity

True sophistication
is usually invisible.

Avoid decoration
that merely demonstrates technical ability.

Prefer restraint.

Prefer clarity.

Prefer silence.

Users should remember ideas,

not effects.

---

# 200. Timelessness

Technology changes.

Frameworks change.

Design trends change.

Programming languages change.

Human curiosity does not.

DeepPhilosophy should therefore pursue
timeless design rather than fashionable design.

If a decision remains elegant ten years later,

it is probably the correct decision.

---

# 201. Responsibility

Every contributor
becomes a temporary curator
of humanity's philosophical heritage.

Write carefully.

Design carefully.

Review carefully.

Respect both the philosophers
and the future readers.

The project is larger
than any individual contributor.

---

# 202. Future

DeepPhilosophy should never be considered finished.

There will always be:

New translations.

New discoveries.

New archaeological evidence.

New interpretations.

New technologies.

The project should remain open
to continuous refinement.

Growth is part of its identity.

---

# 203. Final Principle

If one day a visitor closes the website
remembering only one thing,

let it not be the interface.

Let it not be the animations.

Let it not be the technology.

Let them remember
that across five thousand years,

humanity has never stopped searching for wisdom.

If DeepPhilosophy can quietly communicate
that single truth,

then it has fulfilled its purpose.
